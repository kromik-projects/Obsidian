## The Magic Words

These are the most important phrases in French. Memorize these first.

| French | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| S'il vous plait | seel voo pleh | Please (formal) |
| S'il te plait | seel tuh pleh | Please (informal) |
| Merci | mair-see | Thank you |
| Merci beaucoup | mair-see boh-koo | Thank you very much |
| De rien | duh ree-en | You're welcome (casual) |
| Je vous en prie | zhuh voo-zahn pree | You're welcome (formal) |
| Excusez-moi | ex-kew-zay mwah | Excuse me (formal) |
| Excuse-moi | ex-kewz mwah | Excuse me (informal) |
| Pardon | pahr-dohn | Sorry / Pardon me |
| Je suis desole(e) | zhuh swee day-zoh-lay | I'm sorry |

> [!warning] Important
> **S'il vous plait** is not optional. In France, any request without "s'il vous plait" sounds demanding and rude. Always add it.

## Saying Yes and No

| French | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| Oui | wee | Yes |
| Non | nohn | No |
| Si | see | Yes (contradicting a negative - "You don't like it?" "Si!" = "Yes I do!") |
| Bien sur | bee-en soor | Of course |
| D'accord | dah-kohr | Okay / Agreed |
| Pas du tout | pah dew too | Not at all |
| Peut-etre | puh-teh-truh | Maybe / Perhaps |

> [!tip] "Si" is Special
> If someone asks you a negative question like "Tu n'aimes pas le chocolat ?" (You don't like chocolate?), you say **si** (not oui) to contradict them: "Si, j'adore le chocolat !" (Yes I do, I love chocolate!)

## When You Don't Understand

These phrases will save your life as a beginner:

| French | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| Je ne comprends pas | zhuh nuh kohn-prahn pah | I don't understand |
| Je ne parle pas francais | zhuh nuh pahrl pah frahn-say | I don't speak French |
| Je parle un peu francais | zhuh pahrl uhn puh frahn-say | I speak a little French |
| Parlez plus lentement, s'il vous plait | pahr-lay plew lahnt-mahn seel voo pleh | Speak more slowly, please |
| Pouvez-vous repeter ? | poo-vay voo ray-pay-tay | Can you repeat that? |
| Comment dit-on... en francais ? | koh-mahn dee-tohn... ahn frahn-say | How do you say... in French? |
| Qu'est-ce que ca veut dire ? | kess kuh sah vuh deer | What does that mean? |
| Vous parlez anglais ? | voo pahr-lay ahn-gleh | Do you speak English? |
| Comment ca s'ecrit ? | koh-mahn sah say-kree | How do you spell that? |

> [!tip] Beginner Strategy
> Start interactions with: **"Bonjour ! Je parle un peu francais."** This tells people you are trying and they will usually slow down and be patient with you. French people genuinely appreciate when foreigners make an effort to speak French.

## How French Negation Works (Preview)

You might have noticed something in the phrases above. French negation uses **two words** that wrap around the verb:

**ne... pas** = not

- Je **ne** comprends **pas** = I do **not** understand
- Je **ne** parle **pas** = I do **not** speak
- Je **n'**aime **pas** = I do **not** like (ne becomes n' before a vowel)

> [!note] Spoken French Shortcut
> In casual spoken French, people often drop the "ne":
> - "Je comprends pas" instead of "Je ne comprends pas"
> - "J'aime pas" instead of "Je n'aime pas"
> - This is very common in everyday speech, but you should learn the full form first

## Common Situations - What to Say

### When Someone Helps You
> **Merci beaucoup, c'est tres gentil !**
> *(Thank you very much, that's very kind!)*

### When You Bump Into Someone
> **Oh pardon ! Excusez-moi !**
> *(Oh sorry! Excuse me!)*

### When Entering a Shop
> **Bonjour !**
> *(Hello!)* - Yes, just this. Always say it.

### When Leaving a Shop (Even If You Bought Nothing)
> **Merci, au revoir ! Bonne journee !**
> *(Thank you, goodbye! Have a good day!)*

### When You Need Help
> **Excusez-moi, pouvez-vous m'aider, s'il vous plait ?**
> *(Excuse me, can you help me, please?)*

### When Someone Speaks Too Fast
> **Pardon, je suis debutant. Pouvez-vous parler plus lentement ?**
> *(Sorry, I'm a beginner. Can you speak more slowly?)*
