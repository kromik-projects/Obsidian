## Numbers 21-69

From 21 onwards, you combine tens and ones:

### Tens

| Number | French | Pronunciation |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| 20 | vingt | van |
| 30 | trente | trahnt |
| 40 | quarante | kah-rahnt |
| 50 | cinquante | san-kahnt |
| 60 | soixante | swah-sahnt |

### Combining: Tens + Ones

The rule: tens + ones, connected by a hyphen. BUT when the ones digit is 1, you add **et un** instead:

| Number | French | Pattern |
|--------|--------|---------|
| 21 | vingt et un | 20 "and" 1 |
| 22 | vingt-deux | 20-2 |
| 23 | vingt-trois | 20-3 |
| 31 | trente et un | 30 "and" 1 |
| 35 | trente-cinq | 30-5 |
| 42 | quarante-deux | 40-2 |
| 56 | cinquante-six | 50-6 |
| 69 | soixante-neuf | 60-9 |

## Numbers 70-99: Where French Gets Weird

> [!warning] Brace Yourself
> French counting from 70 to 99 is famously bizarre. Instead of having unique words for 70, 80, and 90, French uses math:
> - **70** = 60 + 10 (soixante-dix)
> - **80** = 4 x 20 (quatre-vingts)
> - **90** = 4 x 20 + 10 (quatre-vingt-dix)

### 70s: Sixty-ten, Sixty-eleven, Sixty-twelve...

| Number | French | Literally |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| 70 | soixante-dix | 60-10 |
| 71 | soixante et onze | 60 and 11 |
| 72 | soixante-douze | 60-12 |
| 73 | soixante-treize | 60-13 |
| 79 | soixante-dix-neuf | 60-19 |

### 80s: Four-twenties, Four-twenties-one...

| Number | French | Literally |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| 80 | quatre-vingts | 4-20s |
| 81 | quatre-vingt-un | 4-20-1 |
| 82 | quatre-vingt-deux | 4-20-2 |
| 89 | quatre-vingt-neuf | 4-20-9 |

> [!note]
> 80 has an "s" on vingts (quatre-vingt**s**), but 81-89 do NOT (quatre-vingt-un, no "s").

### 90s: Four-twenties-ten, Four-twenties-eleven...

| Number | French | Literally |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| 90 | quatre-vingt-dix | 4-20-10 |
| 91 | quatre-vingt-onze | 4-20-11 |
| 92 | quatre-vingt-douze | 4-20-12 |
| 99 | quatre-vingt-dix-neuf | 4-20-19 |

## Days of the Week

| French | Pronunciation | English |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| lundi | luhn-dee | Monday |
| mardi | mahr-dee | Tuesday |
| mercredi | mair-kruh-dee | Wednesday |
| jeudi | zhuh-dee | Thursday |
| vendredi | vahn-druh-dee | Friday |
| samedi | sahm-dee | Saturday |
| dimanche | dee-mahnsh | Sunday |

> [!note] Key Differences from English
> - Days of the week are **not capitalized** in French
> - The French week starts on **Monday** (lundi), not Sunday
> - To say "on Monday," just say **lundi** (no preposition needed): "Je travaille lundi" = "I work on Monday"

| la semaine prochaine | next week |
| la semaine derniere | last week |
| le week-end | the weekend (yes, they use the English word!) |

> [!note]
> Like days, months are **not capitalized** in French.

### Saying Dates

The format in French is: **le + number + month**

- **le 14 juillet** = July 14th (Bastille Day - France's national holiday!)
- **le 25 decembre** = December 25th
- **le 1er janvier** = January 1st (only the 1st uses "premier/1er")

To say "My birthday is...": **Mon anniversaire est le [number] [month].**

## Telling Time

### Asking the Time

| French | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Quelle heure est-il ? | What time is it? (formal) |
| Il est quelle heure ? | What time is it? (casual) |
| Tu as l'heure ? | Do you have the time? (informal) |

### Saying the Time

The structure is: **Il est + [number] + heure(s)**

| Time | French | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1:00 | Il est une heure | "heure" (singular) for 1 o'clock |
| 2:00 | Il est deux heures | "heures" (plural) for everything else |
| 3:15 | Il est trois heures et quart | "and quarter" |
| 4:30 | Il est quatre heures et demie | "and half" |
| 4:45 | Il est cinq heures moins le quart | "5 hours minus a quarter" |
| 12:00 PM | Il est midi | noon |
| 12:00 AM | Il est minuit | midnight |

> [!tip] France Uses 24-Hour Time!
> In daily life and especially in writing, France uses 24-hour time:
> - 14h00 = 2:00 PM
> - 18h30 = 6:30 PM
> - 20h00 = 8:00 PM
>
> In casual speech, people still say "huit heures" for 8 PM if the context is clear.

### Time Phrases

| French | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| le matin | the morning / in the morning |
| l'apres-midi | the afternoon |
| le soir | the evening |
| la nuit | the night |
| tot | early |
| tard | late |
| maintenant | now |
| bientot | soon |
| tout de suite | right away |

## Putting It Together

### Useful Sentences

- **Quel jour sommes-nous ?** = What day is it?
- **Nous sommes lundi.** = It's Monday.
- **On se voit samedi ?** = Shall we meet on Saturday?
- **Le rendez-vous est a quelle heure ?** = What time is the appointment?
- **A huit heures du matin.** = At 8 in the morning.
