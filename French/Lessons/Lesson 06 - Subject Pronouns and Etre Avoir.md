## Subject Pronouns: Who is Doing the Action?

| French | Pronunciation | English | Notes |
|--------|--------------|---------|-------|
| je | zhuh | I | Becomes **j'** before a vowel: j'ai, j'aime |
| tu | tew | you (informal) | One person, someone close to you |
| il | eel | he / it (masc.) | Also used for masculine things |
| elle | ell | she / it (fem.) | Also used for feminine things |
| on | ohn | we / one / people | Very commonly used instead of "nous" in casual speech |
| nous | noo | we | Formal / written |
| vous | voo | you (formal / plural) | One person (formal) OR multiple people |
| ils | eel | they (masc. or mixed) | Group of males, or mixed group |
| elles | ell | they (feminine) | Group of ALL females only |

> [!tip] "On" is Your Best Friend
> In spoken French, **on** is used WAY more than **nous** for "we":
> - **On y va ?** = Shall we go? (You'll hear this constantly)
> - **On mange ?** = Shall we eat?
> - **On** takes the same verb form as **il/elle** (3rd person singular), which makes it easy to conjugate

> [!note] About "Il" and "Elle"
> Just like nouns have gender, "it" in French is either **il** (for masculine nouns) or **elle** (for feminine nouns):
> - Le livre ? **Il** est sur la table. = The book? **It** is on the table.
> - La voiture ? **Elle** est rouge. = The car? **It** (she) is red.

## ETRE - To Be

This is the most important verb in French. It is completely irregular - you just have to memorize it.

### Present Tense Conjugation

| Pronoun | Etre | Pronunciation | English |
|---------|------|--------------|---------|
| je | suis | swee | I am |
| tu | es | ay | you are |
| il / elle / on | est | ay | he/she/one is |
| nous | sommes | sohm | we are |
| vous | etes | ett | you are |
| ils / elles | sont | sohn | they are |

> [!warning] Pronunciation Trap
> **est** (is), **es** (you are), and **et** (and) all sound like "ay." Context tells you which one it is. Don't worry about it - native speakers figure it out by context too.

### Common Uses of Etre

#### 1. Describing People and Things
- **Je suis americain.** = I am American.
- **Elle est belle.** = She is beautiful.
- **Il est grand.** = He is tall.
- **C'est bon.** = It is good. (C'est = ce + est = "it is")
- **Nous sommes contents.** = We are happy.

#### 2. Saying Where Things/People Are
- **Je suis a Paris.** = I am in Paris.
- **Ou est le restaurant ?** = Where is the restaurant?
- **Les cles sont sur la table.** = The keys are on the table.

#### 3. Saying What Time It Is
- **Il est huit heures.** = It is 8 o'clock.

#### 4. Identifying People
- **C'est mon ami.** = This/That is my friend.
- **Ce sont mes parents.** = These are my parents.

## AVOIR - To Have

The second most important verb. Also completely irregular.

### Present Tense Conjugation

| Pronoun | Avoir | Pronunciation | English |
|---------|-------|--------------|---------|
| je | ai | ay | I have |
| tu | as | ah | you have |
| il / elle / on | a | ah | he/she/one has |
| nous | avons | ah-vohn | we have |
| vous | avez | ah-vay | you have |
| ils / elles | ont | ohn | they have |

> [!note] Remember
> **J'ai** (not "je ai") - when **je** comes before a vowel, it drops the E and adds an apostrophe.

### Common Uses of Avoir

#### 1. Possession
- **J'ai un chien.** = I have a dog.
- **Tu as une voiture ?** = Do you have a car?
- **Elle a deux freres.** = She has two brothers.

#### 2. Age (French uses "have" not "be"!)

> [!warning] Major Difference from English
> In English: I **am** 25 years old.
> In French: J'**ai** 25 ans. (Literally: "I **have** 25 years.")

- **J'ai vingt-cinq ans.** = I am 25 years old.
- **Quel age as-tu ?** = How old are you? (Literally: "What age do you have?")
- **Elle a trente ans.** = She is 30 years old.

#### 3. Physical Sensations (French uses "have" not "be"!)
More cases where French says "I **have**" but English says "I **am**":

| French (avoir) | English (to be) |
|---------------|----------------|
| J'ai faim | I am hungry (I "have" hunger) |
| J'ai soif | I am thirsty (I "have" thirst) |
| J'ai froid | I am cold (I "have" cold) |
| J'ai chaud | I am hot (I "have" hot) |
| J'ai sommeil | I am sleepy (I "have" sleep) |
| J'ai peur | I am scared (I "have" fear) |
| J'ai raison | I am right (I "have" reason) |
| J'ai tort | I am wrong (I "have" wrong) |
| J'ai besoin de... | I need... (I "have" need of...) |
| J'ai envie de... | I feel like... (I "have" desire of...) |

## Il y a - There Is / There Are

One of the most useful expressions in French. It uses **avoir**:

**Il y a** (eel ee ah) = There is / There are

- **Il y a un chat ici.** = There is a cat here.
- **Il y a des restaurants pres d'ici.** = There are restaurants near here.
- **Il n'y a pas de probleme.** = There is no problem.
- **Est-ce qu'il y a... ?** = Is there... ?
- **Qu'est-ce qu'il y a ?** = What's wrong? / What's the matter?

## C'est vs. Il est / Elle est

This is a common source of confusion. Here is the simple version:

| Use **C'est** | Use **Il/Elle est** |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Before a noun (with article) | Before an adjective alone |
| C'est **un** ami. (He's a friend.) | Il est **grand**. (He is tall.) |
| C'est **une** bonne idee. (It's a good idea.) | Elle est **belle**. (She is beautiful.) |
| C'est **le** restaurant. (That's the restaurant.) | Il est **francais**. (He is French.) |

> [!tip] Easy Rule
> - **C'est + noun** (with an article: un, une, le, la, mon, etc.)
> - **Il/Elle est + adjective** (no article)

## Negative Forms

To make **etre** and **avoir** negative, wrap them with **ne... pas**:

| Positive | Negative |
|----------|----------|
| Je suis content. | Je **ne** suis **pas** content. |
| J'ai un chien. | Je **n'**ai **pas** de chien. |
| Il est ici. | Il **n'**est **pas** ici. |
| C'est bon. | Ce **n'**est **pas** bon. |
| Il y a du pain. | Il **n'**y a **pas** de pain. |

> [!note] Remember
> After a negative, **un/une/des/du/de la** all become just **de** (or **d'** before a vowel):
> - J'ai **un** chat. -> Je n'ai pas **de** chat.
> - J'ai **des** amis. -> Je n'ai pas **d'**amis.
