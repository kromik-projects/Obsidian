## How French Verbs Work

In English, verbs barely change: "I eat, you eat, we eat, they eat" (only "he eat**s**" changes).

In French, the verb changes for **every** pronoun. This is called **conjugation**.

The good news: there are **patterns**. Most French verbs fall into three groups based on their ending:

| Group | Infinitive Ending | Example | Meaning |
|-------|-------------------|---------|---------|
| 1st group | **-er** | parler | to speak |
| 2nd group | **-ir** | finir | to finish |
| 3rd group | **-re** | vendre | to sell |

> [!tip] The 1st Group (-er) is by Far the Biggest
> About 80% of French verbs end in -er. Learn this pattern well and you can conjugate most verbs.

---

## 1st Group: -ER Verbs

### The Pattern

Take the infinitive, remove **-er**, and add these endings:

| Pronoun | Ending | Example: parler (to speak) |
|---------|--------|---------------------------|
| je | -e | je parl**e** |
| tu | -es | tu parl**es** |
| il/elle/on | -e | il parl**e** |
| nous | -ons | nous parl**ons** |
| vous | -ez | vous parl**ez** |
| ils/elles | -ent | ils parl**ent** |

> [!warning] Pronunciation Secret
> The endings **-e, -es, -ent** are all **silent!** So je parle, tu parles, il parle, and ils parlent all sound EXACTLY the same: "pahrl." Only **nous parlons** and **vous parlez** sound different.

### Examples in Sentences

- **Je parle francais.** = I speak French.
- **Tu manges bien.** = You eat well.
- **Elle travaille a Paris.** = She works in Paris.
- **Nous habitons en France.** = We live in France.
- **Vous regardez la tele ?** = Are you watching TV?
- **Ils jouent au football.** = They play football/soccer.
- **J'aime le chocolat.** = I like/love chocolate.

## 2nd Group: -IR Verbs

### The Pattern

Remove **-ir** and add these endings:

| Pronoun | Ending | Example: finir (to finish) |
|---------|--------|---------------------------|
| je | -is | je fin**is** |
| tu | -is | tu fin**is** |
| il/elle/on | -it | il fin**it** |
| nous | -issons | nous fin**issons** |
| vous | -issez | vous fin**issez** |
| ils/elles | -issent | ils fin**issent** |

> [!note] The -iss- in Plural
> Notice the -iss- that appears in nous, vous, ils/elles forms. This is how you know it is a regular -ir verb (2nd group), not an irregular one.

### Common Regular -IR Verbs

| French | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| finir | to finish |
| choisir | to choose |
| reussir | to succeed |
| remplir | to fill |
| grandir | to grow up |
| rougir | to blush |
| reflechir | to think/reflect |

## 3rd Group: -RE Verbs

### The Pattern

Remove **-re** and add these endings:

| Pronoun | Ending | Example: vendre (to sell) |
|---------|--------|--------------------------|
| je | -s | je vend**s** |
| tu | -s | tu vend**s** |
| il/elle/on | (nothing) | il vend |
| nous | -ons | nous vend**ons** |
| vous | -ez | vous vend**ez** |
| ils/elles | -ent | ils vend**ent** |

### Common Regular -RE Verbs

| French | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| vendre | to sell |
| attendre | to wait (for) |
| entendre | to hear |
| repondre | to answer |
| perdre | to lose |
| descendre | to go down |

## Essential Irregular Verbs

Some of the most used verbs are irregular (they do not follow the patterns above). You already know **etre** and **avoir** from [[Lesson 06 - Subject Pronouns and Etre Avoir]]. Here are more must-know irregular verbs:

### Aller - To Go

| Pronoun | Form | Pronunciation |
|---------|------|--------------|
| je | vais | vay |
| tu | vas | vah |
| il/elle/on | va | vah |
| nous | allons | ah-lohn |
| vous | allez | ah-lay |
| ils/elles | vont | vohn |

**Key uses:**
- **Je vais au restaurant.** = I'm going to the restaurant.
- **Tu vas bien ?** = Are you doing well?
- **On y va !** = Let's go!
- **Comment allez-vous ?** = How are you? (formal)

> [!tip] Aller + Infinitive = Near Future
> **Aller** + another verb in infinitive = "going to [do something]":
> - **Je vais manger.** = I'm going to eat.
> - **On va partir.** = We're going to leave.
> - **Tu vas aimer ca !** = You're going to love this!
>
> This is the easiest way to talk about the future without learning a new tense!

### Faire - To Do / To Make

| Pronoun | Form | Pronunciation |
|---------|------|--------------|
| je | fais | fay |
| tu | fais | fay |
| il/elle/on | fait | fay |
| nous | faisons | fuh-zohn |
| vous | faites | fett |
| ils/elles | font | fohn |

**Key uses:**
- **Qu'est-ce que tu fais ?** = What are you doing?
- **Je fais la cuisine.** = I'm cooking (making food).
- **Il fait beau.** = The weather is nice.
- **Il fait froid.** = It is cold (weather).
- **Ca ne fait rien.** = It doesn't matter.

### Pouvoir - Can / To Be Able To

| Pronoun | Form | Pronunciation |
|---------|------|--------------|
| je | peux | puh |
| tu | peux | puh |
| il/elle/on | peut | puh |
| nous | pouvons | poo-vohn |
| vous | pouvez | poo-vay |
| ils/elles | peuvent | puhv |

**Key uses:**
- **Je peux t'aider.** = I can help you.
- **Tu peux repeter ?** = Can you repeat?
- **On peut y aller.** = We can go (there).

### Vouloir - To Want

| Pronoun | Form | Pronunciation |
|---------|------|--------------|
| je | veux | vuh |
| tu | veux | vuh |
| il/elle/on | veut | vuh |
| nous | voulons | voo-lohn |
| vous | voulez | voo-lay |
| ils/elles | veulent | vuhl |

**Key uses:**
- **Je veux un cafe.** = I want a coffee.
- **Tu veux venir ?** = Do you want to come?
- **Je voudrais...** = I would like... (politer than "je veux")

### Savoir - To Know (Facts/How To)

| Pronoun | Form |
|---------|------|
| je | sais |
| tu | sais |
| il/elle/on | sait |
| nous | savons |
| vous | savez |
| ils/elles | savent |

### Connaitre - To Know (People/Places)

| Pronoun | Form |
|---------|------|
| je | connais |
| tu | connais |
| il/elle/on | connait |
| nous | connaissons |
| vous | connaissez |
| ils/elles | connaissent |

> [!warning] Savoir vs. Connaitre
> Both mean "to know" but are used differently:
> - **Savoir** = knowing facts or how to do something: "Je sais nager" (I know how to swim)
> - **Connaitre** = being familiar with a person/place: "Je connais Paris" (I know Paris)

| French | Meaning |
|--------|---------|

## Practice Exercise

Conjugate the verb in parentheses:

1. Je ___ (parler) anglais.
2. Tu ___ (manger) une pomme.
3. Elle ___ (finir) son travail.
4. Nous ___ (aller) au cinema.
5. Vous ___ (faire) quoi ?
6. Ils ___ (vouloir) partir.
7. Je ___ (pouvoir) t'aider.
8. On ___ (aller) manger. (We're going to eat.)

> [!note]- Answers (click to reveal)
> 1. Je **parle** anglais.
> 2. Tu **manges** une pomme.
> 3. Elle **finit** son travail.
> 4. Nous **allons** au cinema.
> 5. Vous **faites** quoi ?
> 6. Ils **veulent** partir.
> 7. Je **peux** t'aider.
> 8. On **va** manger.
