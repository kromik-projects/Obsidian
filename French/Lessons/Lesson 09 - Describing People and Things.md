## Adjectives in French: Two Key Rules

French adjectives work differently from English in two important ways:

### Rule 1: Adjectives Must Agree in Gender and Number

Adjectives change their form to match the noun they describe:

| | Masculine | Feminine | Masc. Plural | Fem. Plural |
|---|-----------|----------|-------------|------------|
| small | petit | petit**e** | petit**s** | petit**es** |
| tall | grand | grand**e** | grand**s** | grand**es** |
| intelligent | intelligent | intelligent**e** | intelligent**s** | intelligent**es** |

**The basic pattern:**
- Feminine: add **-e**
- Plural: add **-s**
- Feminine plural: add **-es**

Examples:
- **un petit chat** = a small cat (masculine)
- **une petite maison** = a small house (feminine)
- **des petits chats** = small cats (masc. plural)
- **des petites maisons** = small houses (fem. plural)

> [!note] If the adjective already ends in -e, it does not change for feminine:
> - **Il est jeune.** / **Elle est jeune.** (young - same form)
> - **Il est triste.** / **Elle est triste.** (sad - same form)

### Rule 2: Most Adjectives Come AFTER the Noun

This is the opposite of English:

| English (adj + noun) | French (noun + adj) |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| a red car | une voiture **rouge** |
| an interesting book | un livre **interessant** |
| a French woman | une femme **francaise** |

### The BANGS Exceptions (Adjectives That Go BEFORE)

A small group of common short adjectives go **before** the noun. Remember them with the acronym **BANGS**:

| Category | Adjective | Meaning |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| **B**eauty | beau/belle | beautiful |
| | joli(e) | pretty |
| **A**ge | jeune | young |
| | vieux/vieille | old |
| | nouveau/nouvelle | new |
| **N**umber | premier/premiere | first |
| | dernier/derniere | last |
| **G**oodness | bon(ne) | good |
| | mauvais(e) | bad |
| | gentil(le) | kind/nice |
| **S**ize | petit(e) | small |
| | grand(e) | big/tall |
| | gros(se) | fat/big |
| | long(ue) | long |
| | court(e) | short |

Examples:
- **une belle femme** = a beautiful woman (before)
- **un petit enfant** = a small child (before)
- **un bon restaurant** = a good restaurant (before)
- But: **une voiture rouge** = a red car (after)

## Irregular Adjective Forms

Some common adjectives have irregular feminine forms:

| Masculine | Feminine | Meaning |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| beau | belle | beautiful |
| nouveau | nouvelle | new |
| vieux | vieille | old |
| bon | bonne | good |
| gros | grosse | fat/big |
| long | longue | long |
| blanc | blanche | white |
| gentil | gentille | kind |
| fou | folle | crazy |
| doux | douce | sweet/soft |
| heureux | heureuse | happy |
| sportif | sportive | sporty |

> [!note] Special Masculine Forms Before Vowels
> **Beau**, **nouveau**, and **vieux** have special forms before masculine nouns starting with a vowel:
> - un **bel** homme (a handsome man)
> - un **nouvel** ami (a new friend)
> - un **vieil** arbre (an old tree)

### How to Express Your Emotions

Use **etre** (to be) + emotion:

- **Je suis content.** = I am happy.
- **Elle est triste.** = She is sad.
- **Nous sommes fatigues.** = We are tired.
- **Tu es en colere ?** = Are you angry?
- **Je suis amoureux de toi.** = I am in love with you. (male speaker)
- **Je suis amoureuse de toi.** = I am in love with you. (female speaker)

## Useful Comparison Words

| French | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| plus... que | more... than | Il est **plus grand que** moi. (He is taller than me.) |
| moins... que | less... than | C'est **moins cher que** l'autre. (It's cheaper than the other.) |
| aussi... que | as... as | Elle est **aussi belle que** sa mere. (She's as beautiful as her mother.) |
| le/la plus | the most | C'est **le plus** beau. (It's the most beautiful.) |

## Putting Descriptions Together

### Template for Describing Someone

> **Il/Elle est [physical] avec les cheveux [hair] et les yeux [eyes]. Il/Elle est [personality].**

Example:
> **Ma copine est grande et mince avec les cheveux longs et bruns et les yeux verts. Elle est tres intelligente et drole.**
> *(My girlfriend is tall and slim with long brown hair and green eyes. She is very intelligent and funny.)*

### Template for Describing Things

> **C'est un(e) [size] [noun] [color]. Il/Elle est [quality].**

Example:
> **C'est une grande maison blanche. Elle est tres belle.**
> *(It's a big white house. It is very beautiful.)*
