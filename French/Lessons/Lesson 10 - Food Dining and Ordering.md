## French Food Culture - What You Need to Know

> [!info] Cultural Context
> Food is **central** to French life. Meals are longer than in the US, lunch breaks can be 1-2 hours, and meals are social events. Rushing through food is considered strange. When dining with French people, take your time and enjoy the experience.

Key cultural points:
- **Lunch** is traditionally the biggest meal (though this is changing in cities)
- **Dinner** is usually around 7:30-8:30 PM (later than many countries)
- Bread is served with every meal and it goes directly on the table, not on a plate
- You tear bread with your hands, never cut it with a knife
- Always say **Bonjour** when entering a restaurant
- Wait to be seated - don't just grab a table

## At the Restaurant - Key Phrases

### Arriving

| French | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Bonjour, une table pour deux, s'il vous plait. | Hello, a table for two, please. |
| Avez-vous une reservation ? | Do you have a reservation? |
| J'ai reserve au nom de... | I have a reservation under the name... |
| On peut s'asseoir en terrasse ? | Can we sit on the terrace? |
| On peut s'asseoir a l'interieur ? | Can we sit inside? |

### Ordering

| French | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| La carte, s'il vous plait. | The menu, please. |
| Qu'est-ce que vous recommandez ? | What do you recommend? |
| Quel est le plat du jour ? | What is the dish of the day? |
| Je voudrais... | I would like... |
| Je vais prendre... | I'll have... |
| Pour moi, le/la... | For me, the... |
| Et comme boisson ? | And to drink? |
| Une carafe d'eau, s'il vous plait. | A carafe of water, please. (free tap water) |
| C'est quoi exactement, le/la... ? | What exactly is the...? |
| Je suis allergique a... | I am allergic to... |
| Je suis vegetarien(ne). | I am vegetarian. |
| C'est sans gluten ? | Is it gluten-free? |

### During the Meal

| French | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| C'est delicieux ! | It's delicious! |
| C'est tres bon ! | It's very good! |
| Encore un peu de pain, s'il vous plait. | A little more bread, please. |
| Excusez-moi... | Excuse me... (to call the waiter) |

### Paying

| French | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| L'addition, s'il vous plait. | The check, please. |
| On peut payer ? | Can we pay? |
| Je peux payer par carte ? | Can I pay by card? |
| On partage ? | Shall we split it? |
| C'est pour moi. / Je t'invite. | It's on me. / My treat. |

> [!tip] Tipping in France
> The tip is already **included** in the price in France (service compris). You don't need to add 15-20% like in the US. It is normal to leave a small extra amount (1-2 euros) for good service, but it is not expected or required.

> [!tip] Getting Free Water
> Ask for **une carafe d'eau** (a carafe of water) - this is free tap water. If you just say "de l'eau," they might bring expensive bottled water.

## Understanding a French Menu

A typical French menu has these sections:

| French | English | What It Is |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Les entrees | Starters/Appetizers | First course (NOT "entree" like in American English!) |
| Les plats (principaux) | Main courses | The main dish |
| Les desserts | Desserts | Sweet course |
| Les boissons | Drinks | Beverages |
| Le menu / La formule | Set menu | A fixed price for 2-3 courses (usually a great deal) |
| A la carte | A la carte | Order individual dishes |

> [!warning] "Entree" Means Starter!
> In American English, "entree" means the main course. In French, **l'entree** is the STARTER. The main course is **le plat principal**.

## At a Cafe

French cafes are a way of life. Here is what you need to know:

| French | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Un cafe | An espresso (this is the default "coffee" in France!) |
| Un cafe creme | Espresso with steamed milk (like a latte) |
| Un cafe allonge | A longer coffee (like American filter coffee) |
| Un cafe noisette | Espresso with a dash of milk |
| Un decafeine / un deca | Decaf |
| Un the | A tea |
| Un chocolat chaud | A hot chocolate |
| Un croissant | A croissant |
| Un pain au chocolat | Chocolate croissant |
| Une tartine | Bread with butter and/or jam |

> [!note] Morning vs. Afternoon Coffee
> **Cafe creme** or anything milky is traditionally a **morning** drink. Ordering one after a meal may get you an odd look. After lunch or dinner, order **un cafe** (espresso).

### How to Order at a Bakery

> **You:** Bonjour !
> **Baker:** Bonjour ! Qu'est-ce que je vous sers ?
> **You:** Je voudrais une baguette et deux croissants, s'il vous plait.
> **Baker:** Et avec ceci ? (Anything else?)
> **You:** C'est tout, merci. (That's all, thank you.)
> **Baker:** Ca fait trois euros cinquante. (That's 3.50 euros.)
> **You:** Voila. Merci, bonne journee !
> **Baker:** Merci, bonne journee !

## Practice Dialogue: Ordering at a Restaurant

> **Serveur:** Bonjour ! Vous avez choisi ?
> *(Hello! Have you decided?)*
>
> **You:** Oui. Comme entree, je vais prendre la soupe a l'oignon.
> *(Yes. For a starter, I'll have the onion soup.)*
>
> **Serveur:** Et comme plat principal ?
> *(And for the main course?)*
>
> **You:** Le poulet roti avec des haricots verts, s'il vous plait.
> *(The roasted chicken with green beans, please.)*
>
> **Serveur:** Tres bien. Et comme boisson ?
> *(Very good. And to drink?)*
>
> **You:** Un verre de vin rouge, s'il vous plait. Et une carafe d'eau.
> *(A glass of red wine, please. And a carafe of water.)*
>
> **Serveur:** Parfait.
> *(Perfect.)*
>
> *[After the meal]*
>
> **You:** C'etait delicieux ! L'addition, s'il vous plait.
> *(It was delicious! The check, please.)*
