## Step-by-Step Stem Christie

### Turning Left

1. **Traverse** or glide with skis relatively parallel
2. **Stem out** the right ski (push tail out, creating a wedge)
3. **Weight the right ski** (the stemmed ski becomes your turning ski)
4. **Begin the turn** - you'll start curving left
5. **Match the skis** - bring left ski parallel to right ski
6. **Complete turn** with both skis parallel, crossing the fall line
7. **Edge and stop** or begin next turn

## The "Match" - Most Important Part

The moment you bring the inside ski parallel to the outside ski is called the "match." This is the skill that transitions you from wedge to parallel.

### Timing the Match

| Match Timing | Result | When to Use |
|--------------|--------|-------------|
| Late (after turn) | Safer, more control | Learning, steeper terrain |
| Middle (during turn) | Balanced | Most situations |
| Early (start of turn) | More dynamic | As skill improves |

### Practicing the Match

**Drill: Exaggerated Match**
1. Start in wedge
2. Begin turn
3. **Loudly/actively bring inside ski in** (make it obvious)
4. Feel the skis come parallel
5. Repeat other direction

## Common Stem Christie Mistakes

### 1. Forgetting to Weight the Outside Ski

**Problem:** Turn feels weak, skis don't respond
**Feel:** Like you're just twisting, not turning

**Fix:** Really commit weight to the stemmed (outside) ski. You should feel 70-80% of weight there.

### 2. Never Actually Matching

**Problem:** Staying in wedge through entire turn
**Feel:** Legs get tired, turns feel effortful

**Fix:** Consciously pull that inside ski in. Say "match!" out loud as you do it.

### 3. Matching Too Early (Before the Fall Line)

**Problem:** Losing control, speeding up
**Feel:** Scary, out of control

**Fix:** Be patient. Keep the wedge until you're past the fall line, then match.

**Problem:** Shoulders twisting into turn
**Feel:** Awkward, off-balance

**Fix:** Keep chest facing downhill. Turn with legs, not torso.

## Drills for Stem Christie

### Drill 1: Stem-Match Garland

On a gentle slope:
1. Traverse across
2. Stem the downhill ski briefly
3. Match back to parallel
4. Repeat without actually completing a full turn

This isolates the stem-match feeling.

### Drill 2: Hockey Stop Practice

From a slow traverse, try to stop with both skis parallel (hockey stop style). This builds matching skills.

### Drill 3: One Turn, Then Stop

Make ONE stem christie turn, then stop completely in a parallel position. Reset and repeat. Quality over quantity.

### Drill 4: Counting

Count "1-2-3-MATCH" during each turn:
- 1 = Stem out
- 2 = Weight outside ski
- 3 = Begin turn
- MATCH = Bring skis parallel

### Drill 5: Follow the Leader

Ski behind a more experienced skier, trying to match their turn shape and timing.

| Building consistency | Moderate green | Link 5+ stem christies |

| Refining | Blue runs | Smooth, controlled stem christies |
