# Parallel Turn Basics - Both Skis Together

The parallel turn is when skiing starts to feel like "real" skiing. Both skis stay parallel throughout the entire turn. This is your goal!

In a parallel turn:
- Skis remain hip-width apart

- Both skis arc through the turn together
- No wedge needed at any point

### The Four Key Moments

1. **Initiation** - Start the turn by flattening skis and shifting weight
2. **Fall Line** - Skis point downhill, maximum speed moment
3. **Apex** - Maximum edge angle, skis carving through turn
4. **Completion** - Turn finishes, prepare for next turn

## How to Make a Parallel Turn

### Step-by-Step (Turning Left)

1. **Start in athletic stance**, skis parallel, traversing or pointing slightly downhill

2. **Extend/rise slightly** - This lightens the skis and releases the edges

3. **Flatten your skis** - Roll ankles from current edges to flat

4. **Transfer weight to right (outside) ski** - Feel pressure on that foot

5. **Roll both ankles left** - This tips both skis onto their left edges

6. **Point knees into the turn** - Knees face where you want to go

7. **Let skis arc** - The sidecut of the ski creates the turn

8. **Control radius** - More edge angle = tighter turn

9. **Finish across the slope** - Bleed speed by completing turn

10. **Prepare for next turn** - Repeat on other side

## Common Parallel Turn Mistakes

### 1. Rotating Upper Body âŒ

**Problem:** Whole body twists into turn
**Looks like:** Shoulders and chest turn with skis
**Fix:** Keep chest facing downhill, let legs work independently

### 2. Not Enough Weight on Outside Ski âŒ

**Problem:** Skis skid, don't carve
**Feel:** Slipping sideways instead of arcing
**Fix:** Really commit to that outside ski (pick up inside ski slightly to test)

### 3. Too Much Weight on Inside Ski âŒ

**Problem:** Inside ski catches, crossed tips
**Looks like:** Inside ski runs away
**Fix:** Lighten inside ski, could lift it briefly

### 4. Leaning Into the Hill (Banking) âŒ

**Problem:** Whole body tips like a motorcycle
**Why bad:** Reduces edge grip, easy to wash out
**Fix:** **Angulate** - keep upper body upright while legs tip

## Parallel Turn Drills

### Drill 1: Javelin Turns

Lift your inside ski completely off the snow during turns. This FORCES you to balance on the outside ski.

```
Javelin Turn:

    â—‹
   /|\
    |                Inside ski lifted!
   / \
  /   \---------
 â•±
```

### Drill 2: 1000 Steps

Make very short turns by stepping your skis through the turn (like walking). This builds the feeling of independent leg movement.

### Drill 3: Railroad Tracks

Try to leave two clean lines in the snow (like railroad tracks), not a smeared fan shape. This indicates clean carving.

### Drill 4: Shuffle Turns

Keep feet moving, stepping weight from ski to ski through turns. Prevents getting stuck on one ski.

### Drill 5: Pole Drag

Drag the pole tip on the inside of your turn. This creates upper/lower body separation and proper body position.

### Drill 6: Hands on Hips

Ski with hands on hips. Forces you to use legs for turning, not arm swinging.

## Building Parallel Turn Consistency

### Progression

1. **Stem parallel** - Start with small stem, match immediately
2. **Parallel with traverse** - Make one turn, traverse, turn again
3. **Linked parallel** - Turn after turn without stopping
4. **Rhythmic parallel** - Smooth, connected, consistent timing

### Terrain Progression

| Stage | Terrain | Focus |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Learning | Easy green | Get the feeling |
| Building | Green/easy blue | Link turns |
| Refining | Blue | Speed control, consistency |
| Testing | Steeper blue | Confidence in the skill |

## Key Takeaway

> **Parallel skiing is about EDGES and WEIGHT, not about twisting or forcing.**

Let your skis do the work. Tip them on edge, weight the outside ski, and they will turn. The more you fight it, the harder it is.
