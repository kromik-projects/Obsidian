## Speed Control Technique: The Check

A "check" is a quick edge-set to scrub speed between turns.

### How to Check

1. Coming out of a turn, set edges firmly
2. Push against the snow briefly
3. Flex and extend to release
4. Begin next turn

## Mental Techniques for Speed

### 1. Breathe

Holding breath = tense body = poor skiing
Exhale through turns = relaxed skiing

### 2. Look Ahead

Looking at ski tips = reactive = feel fast
Looking 2-3 turns ahead = proactive = feel controlled

### 3. Count Your Rhythm

"1-2-3-turn, 1-2-3-turn" creates rhythm and prevents freezing.

### 4. Pick Your Line

Plan where your next 3 turns will be. Having a plan feels controlled.

## Terrain-Specific Speed Control

### On Groomed Runs

- Turn shape is your friend
- Carving edges grip well
- Long turns for speed, short turns for control

### On Ice

- **Reduce edge angle** (less likely to slip out)
- **Lighter, quicker turns**
- Stay forward in stance
- Don't fight it - flow with available grip

### On Steep Terrain

- **Finish turns fully** (across the slope)
- Keep turns connected
- No straight running between turns
- Use checks if needed

### In Crud/Chopped Snow

- Medium turn radius
- More active leg movements
- Absorb variations with legs
- Moderate speed (not too slow!)

**Why it happens:** Fear â†’ want to "brake"
**What happens:** Skis accelerate away, MORE out of control
**Fix:** Get forward! Commit to the turn.

### 2. Looking at the Steepest Part âŒ

**Why:** Your eyes go to the scary thing
**What happens:** You ski toward it (you go where you look!)
**Fix:** Look where you want to go - across the slope

### 3. Not Finishing Turns âŒ

**Why:** Want to hurry through the scary part
**What happens:** Speed builds each turn
**Fix:** Be patient, let each turn come across the slope

### 4. Going Stiff âŒ

**Why:** Fear = tension
**What happens:** Legs can't absorb, catch edges, crash
**Fix:** Stay bouncy, move with the terrain

**Why:** Wedge feels like brakes
**What happens:** At higher speeds, wedge is unstable

## Building Speed Confidence

### Drill 1: Speed Runs on Easy Terrain

Go faster than comfortable on GREEN runs. This teaches that speed isn't inherently dangerous.

### Drill 2: Speed Control on Moderate Terrain

On BLUE runs, practice going slow, medium, fast - all by controlling turn shape, not braking.

### Drill 3: Countdown Stops

Pick a spot ahead. Count "5-4-3-2-1-stop" and stop exactly there. Builds confidence in your control.

### Drill 4: Emergency Stop Practice

From moderate speed, practice quick parallel stops (hockey stops). Know you CAN stop quickly.

### Drill 5: Follow Someone Faster

Ski behind a more skilled friend. Try to match their line and speed. Shows you what's possible.

## Emergency Speed Situations

### If Going Too Fast

1. **Don't panic** - tensing makes it worse
2. **Turn across the slope** - commit to a full turn
3. **Sit into the turn** - dig edges in hard
4. **If needed, fall** - controlled fall is better than collision

### Falling Safely

If you must fall:
- Fall **uphill** (toward the slope)
- Fall on your **side/hip**, not forward
- Keep arms in (not outstretched)
- Slide to a stop
