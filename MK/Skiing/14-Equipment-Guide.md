## BOOTS - The Most Important Equipment

### Why Boots Matter Most

- They're your connection to the ski
- Poor fit = poor control, pain, frustration
- Good fit = everything feels easier

### Boot Fit Checklist

| Good Fit âœ… | Bad Fit âŒ |
|-------------|-----------|
| Snug all around | Loose, foot moves |
| Heel stays down | Heel lifts when flexing |
| Toes lightly touch (standing) | Toes crammed or lots of room |
| Toes pull back when flexed forward | Toes always smashed |
| No pressure points after 10 min | Pain spots anywhere |
| Comfortable all day | Agony by lunch |

**For a beginner with 2 weeks experience:** Look for 60-90 flex

**Note:** Women's boots are generally 10-20 points softer at same flex number.

### Boot Fitting Tips

1. **Go to a specialty ski shop**, not a big box store
2. **Try boots in the afternoon** (feet swell during day)
3. **Wear thin ski socks** (not thick cotton)
4. **Spend time in boots** - walk around for 15+ minutes
5. **Consider custom footbeds** - they make a huge difference

### Common Boot Problems

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Shin pain ("shin bang") | Leaning back | Better stance + boot fit |
| Numb toes | Too tight or cold | Loosen buckles, warmer socks |
| Heel lift | Boot too big | Different boot / heel shims |
| Arch pain | No support | Custom footbeds |
| Ankle pain | Pressure point | Boot punch / different boot |

## BINDINGS

### What Bindings Do

1. **Connect you to ski**
2. **Release when you fall** (safety!)
3. **Transfer energy** from boots to skis

### DIN Setting

### Base Layer
- **Material**: Merino wool or synthetic (NOT cotton!)
- **Fit**: Snug but not restrictive
- **Purpose**: Wick sweat away from skin

### Mid Layer
- **Material**: Fleece, down, or synthetic insulation
- **Fit**: Comfortable, allows movement
- **Purpose**: Trap warm air

### Outer Layer (Jacket/Pants)
- **Waterproof rating**: 10,000mm+ minimum
- **Breathability**: 10,000g+ minimum
- **Features**: Powder skirt, vents, pockets

### What to Buy First

3. **Skis + Bindings** - After boots dialed
4. **Poles** - Last (cheap, don't matter much)

## Equipment Care

### Daily Care
- Dry boot liners overnight (remove if possible)
- Wipe snow off skis before storage
- Store skis bases together, edges out

### Seasonal Care
- Wax skis (or have shop do it)
- Check edges for nicks/rust
- Check boot soles for wear
- Have bindings tested annually

### Storage
- Clean and dry everything
- Apply storage wax to skis
- Loosen boot buckles
- Store in cool, dry place (not hot garage)
