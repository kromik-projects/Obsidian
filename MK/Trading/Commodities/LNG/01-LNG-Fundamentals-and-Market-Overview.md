# LNG Fundamentals & Market Overview

## What is LNG?

**Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG)** is natural gas (predominantly methane, CH₄) that has been cooled to approximately **-162°C (-260°F)** at atmospheric pressure, converting it from a gaseous state to a liquid state. This process reduces the volume of natural gas by approximately **600 times**, making it economically viable to transport over long distances where pipeline infrastructure is impractical or non-existent.

### Chemical Composition

| Component | Typical Range |
|-----------|--------------|
| Methane (CH₄) | 85-97% |
| Ethane (C₂H₆) | 2-8% |
| Propane (C₃H₈) | 0.5-2% |
| Butane (C₄H₁₀) | 0.1-1% |
| Nitrogen (N₂) | 0-1% |
| CO₂ | <50 ppm |
| H₂S | <4 ppm |

The exact composition varies by source and affects the **heating value** (energy content) and **Wobbe Index** (interchangeability metric).

## The LNG Value Chain

Understanding the LNG value chain is critical for trading as each segment represents distinct risk profiles and value capture opportunities.

### 1. Upstream (Exploration & Production)
- Gas field development
- Production optimization
- Feed gas treatment (removal of CO₂, H₂S, water, heavy hydrocarbons)

### 2. Liquefaction
- **Capital intensive**: $1,000-2,000+ per tonne of annual capacity
- **Major technologies**:
  - **APCI (Air Products)**: C3MR, AP-X processes (~80% market share)
  - **ConocoPhillips**: Optimized Cascade
  - **Shell**: DMR (Dual Mixed Refrigerant)
  - **Linde**: MFC (Mixed Fluid Cascade)

- **Liquefaction capacity utilization** is a key market indicator

### 3. Shipping
- LNG carriers (LNGCs) transport cargoes globally
- Ship types: Conventional, Q-Flex, Q-Max, small-scale
- Boil-off management (BOG)
- Chartering: time charter, voyage charter, spot

### 4. Regasification
- Onshore terminals
- Floating Storage and Regasification Units (FSRUs)
- Send-out capacity and flexibility

### 5. Downstream Distribution
- Pipeline injection
- Industrial/power sector delivery
- Small-scale distribution (trucking, bunkering)

## Market Structure Evolution

### Traditional Market (Pre-2010)
- **Long-term contracts** dominated (20-25 year terms)
- **Oil-indexed pricing** (JCC in Asia, Brent in Europe)
- **Destination clauses** (cargo restrictions)
- **Take-or-pay obligations** (typically 85-90%)
- **Point-to-point** supply arrangements
- Limited secondary trading

### Modern Market (2015-Present)
- **Rise of spot and short-term trading** (now ~35-40% of trade)
- **Portfolio players** emerge (Shell, TotalEnergies, BP, Chevron)
- **Hub-based pricing** gaining traction (JKM, TTF, Henry Hub)
- **Destination flexibility** increasingly standard
- **Derivatives markets** mature (futures, swaps, options)
- **LNG as a global commodity** rather than regional product

### Key Structural Shifts
1. **US LNG exports** (2016+) with Henry Hub-linked pricing and FOB destination-free cargoes
2. **European energy crisis** (2022) accelerating gas hub liquidity
3. **Asian buyers** seeking diversification and flexibility
4. **Financialization** of LNG markets

## Supply-Demand Dynamics

### Supply-Side Factors
- **Project FIDs (Final Investment Decisions)**: 4-6 year lag to production
- **Liquefaction utilization rates**: Maintenance, feed gas availability
- **Shipping availability**: Fleet size, distances, canal access
- **Geopolitical risks**: Sanctions, conflicts, contract disputes
- **Weather**: Hurricanes (US Gulf), extreme cold (Russia, Australia)

### Demand-Side Factors
- **Weather**: Heating demand (winter), cooling demand (summer)
- **Economic growth**: Industrial activity, GDP
- **Power sector fuel switching**: Coal-to-gas, gas-to-coal prices
- **Nuclear availability**: Outages impact gas demand
- **Renewable penetration**: Displacement vs. backup needs
- **Storage levels**: Europe, Asia storage dynamics
- **Currency movements**: Local affordability in importing countries

### Seasonality Patterns

| Season | Northern Hemisphere | Southern Hemisphere |
|--------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Q1 (Jan-Mar) | Peak heating demand (Asia, Europe) | Shoulder |
| Q2 (Apr-Jun) | Shoulder, storage injection starts | Autumn, moderate demand |
| Q3 (Jul-Sep) | Summer cooling (Japan, Korea), European injection | Winter heating (S. America) |
| Q4 (Oct-Dec) | Pre-winter stocking, early heating | Spring shoulder |

| **FID** | Final Investment Decision |
| **FSRU** | Floating Storage and Regasification Unit |

## Why LNG Markets Matter for Traders

1. **Price Volatility**: LNG prices can swing dramatically (2021-2022: $5 to $70/MMBtu)
2. **Arbitrage Opportunities**: Regional price spreads (Asia vs. Europe vs. US)
3. **Optionality Value**: Destination flexibility, timing, shipping
4. **Geopolitical Significance**: Energy security, transition fuel
5. **Growing Liquidity**: More trading participants, derivatives
6. **Complexity Premium**: Specialized knowledge creates edge
